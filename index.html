    Probabilities

    "The probability of any outcome from a random phenomenom is the proportion of times the outcome would occur in a very long series of repetitions."

    Theoretical probability:
    "Logical Probability"

    Theoretical probabilities are calculated through mathematicaly logical reasoning to obtain a theoretical approximation of the result of a long series of repeteted test.

    Experimental probability:
    "Field-Tested Probability"

    Experimental probabilities are caulculated through repeated and controlled test which constrast a possible pattern giving an overall probability.
